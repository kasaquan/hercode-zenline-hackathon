# Challenge Brief

## Mission

Build a reusable system that detects emerging retail opportunities from global and local market signals.

Use a Swiss outdoor retailer as the shared challenge scenario. Your system should identify promising outdoor-retail opportunities, explain where each opportunity appears first, assess whether it can transfer into Switzerland or DACH, and recommend what the retailer should do next.

## Why This Case Matters

Outdoor retail is a perfect test case because the market moves across culture, climate, technology, and lifestyle at the same time. A useful opportunity might be a rising brand, a growing product type, a new material, a technical feature, a price-band gap, a marketplace bestseller, or a local Swiss weather-driven need.

Retailers need this kind of system because waiting for clean sales history is often too late. By the time a trend is obvious in internal data, competitors may already own the category, supplier access, or customer mindshare.

## What Counts As An Opportunity

An opportunity can be a:

- Product type.
- Product feature or material.
- Usage occasion.
- Service or rental model.
- Merchandising idea.
- Brand or supplier lead.
- Price or assortment gap.
- Content or community angle.

Before building, ask what an outdoor retailer is most interested in:

- Which brands are gaining momentum and might be worth scouting?
- Which product types are appearing first in other markets?
- Which materials, technologies, or features are moving from niche to mainstream?
- Which local assortment gaps are competitors not covering well?
- Which opportunities could improve margin, differentiation, loyalty, or supplier access?
- Which signals are strong enough to justify a test, buy, launch, or monitoring workflow?

Then design a methodology around those questions. Your implementation should show how you gather evidence, compare sources, score or prioritize signals, and translate the result into a retailer-facing recommendation.

## Recommended Flow

1. Gather market signals from several source types.
2. Normalize the evidence into structured rows.
3. Deduplicate repeated signals across markets and sources.
4. Score each candidate opportunity.
5. Assess Swiss/DACH transferability.
6. Convert the best candidates into business actions.
7. Show the result in a dashboard, report, API, notebook, or other demo-ready format.

## Quality Bar

For each recommended opportunity, preserve:

- Source names and URLs.
- Market or geography.
- Why it is emerging now.
- Signal strength and limitations.
- Transferability reasoning for Switzerland or DACH.
- Recommended next action.
- Confidence, risks, and missing evidence.

Separate directional signals from hard commercial proof. Weak evidence should trigger more research rather than overconfident recommendations.
